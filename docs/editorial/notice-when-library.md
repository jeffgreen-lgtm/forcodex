# Notice-When Library

See also: [Field Guide](./field-guide.md), [Headline Library](./headline-library.md), [Your-Move Library](./your-move-library.md), [Editorial Rules](./editorial-rules.md), [Taxonomy](./taxonomy.md)

## Rules for Strong Notice-When Moments

- Use observable, specific moments.
- Avoid vague emotions with no context.
- Avoid prediction language.
- Prefer scenes, habits, interruptions, and repeated situations.
- Keep each example close to normal daily life.

## Editorial Status

All examples below are initial editorial examples, not final production copy.

## Communication

- Notice when you rewrite the same message three times.
- Notice when a simple reply starts turning into a long defense.
- Notice when a conversation gets easier after one direct question.
- Notice when you almost answer before you fully understand the point.
- Notice when silence gives you more information than another explanation.

## Relationships

- Notice when you start managing someone else’s mood for them.
- Notice when a small delay feels bigger than it is.
- Notice when care turns into over-monitoring.
- Notice when the conversation improves after one honest sentence.
- Notice when you want reassurance but ask for control instead.

## Confidence

- Notice when you overstate certainty to calm your own nerves.
- Notice when your posture changes after one clear decision.
- Notice when you know what you think before you say it.
- Notice when you start explaining your worth instead of using it.
- Notice when quiet competence lands better than emphasis.

## Decisions

- Notice when a choice gets muddier the more people you poll.
- Notice when waiting 20 minutes improves the answer.
- Notice when the “urgent” option keeps changing shape.
- Notice when the simplest option also feels cleanest in your body.
- Notice when the hard part is choosing, not understanding.

## Work

- Notice when email starts replacing actual progress.
- Notice when one unfinished task keeps shadowing the rest of the list.
- Notice when your best focus arrives after you close extra tabs.
- Notice when the work gets easier after naming the real priority.
- Notice when you are busy but not advancing.

## Creativity

- Notice when the idea improves once you stop trying to impress.
- Notice when a rough draft creates more momentum than more planning.
- Notice when the work comes alive in one small corner first.
- Notice when perfection stalls a promising start.
- Notice when curiosity works better than pressure.

## Rest

- Notice when normal tasks feel louder than they should.
- Notice when your attention frays after too many small demands.
- Notice when the better answer appears after a short pause.
- Notice when fatigue is masquerading as irritability.
- Notice when your body wants a break before your schedule agrees.

## Boundaries

- Notice when you say yes before checking your own capacity.
- Notice when resentment arrives before the task even begins.
- Notice when a quick answer opens a longer obligation.
- Notice when you are explaining a limit that only needs stating.
- Notice when one person keeps treating your availability as open access.

## Growth

- Notice when the same problem keeps appearing in a new outfit.
- Notice when your resistance is stronger than the actual task.
- Notice when the old response no longer feels believable.
- Notice when change gets easier after one truthful admission.
- Notice when the lesson becomes obvious after the pattern repeats.

## Home

- Notice when one messy area affects the tone of the whole room.
- Notice when the house feels heavier because nothing has been reset.
- Notice when one small repair keeps asking for attention.
- Notice when a calmer surface changes your mood immediately.
- Notice when the atmosphere improves after one practical fix.

## Money

- Notice when spending rises with stress, not need.
- Notice when avoidance makes the numbers feel scarier than they are.
- Notice when one skipped check-in creates confusion later.
- Notice when the clearer choice is the less emotional one.
- Notice when your plan works better after you simplify it.

## Purpose

- Notice when your energy rises around one specific kind of task.
- Notice when distraction increases near meaningful work.
- Notice when you feel clearer after doing the obvious next thing.
- Notice when ambition gets vague because your focus is split.
- Notice when the right direction feels quieter than the fantasy.
