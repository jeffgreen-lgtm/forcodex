# Editorial Rules

See also: [Field Guide](./field-guide.md), [Headline Library](./headline-library.md), [Notice-When Library](./notice-when-library.md), [Your-Move Library](./your-move-library.md), [Taxonomy](./taxonomy.md), [CosmoScope Canon](../cosmoscope-canon.md), [Reading Pipeline](../reading-pipeline.md)

## Core Rules

- Prepare, do not predict.
- Astrology is the source of truth.
- Recognition builds trust.
- The answer comes before the explanation.
- Every sentence must earn its place.
- Concrete over abstract.
- One primary story per reading.
- Preserve agency.
- No fear-based engagement.
- No therapy jargon.
- No generic AI language.
- No mystical clichés.

## Headline Test

- Is it under 12 words?
- Does it express one useful human truth?
- Does it avoid planet names, sign names, and atmospheric filler?
- Would a real user recognize the truth of it immediately?

## Notice-When Test

- Is it observable in ordinary life?
- Is it specific enough to picture?
- Does it avoid prediction language?
- Does it point to a moment, not a vague feeling?

## Your-Move Test

- Is it one action?
- Can it be done in under five minutes?
- Is it concrete and behavioral?
- Does it avoid empty mindset advice?

## Astrology Fidelity Test

- Does the copy stay inside the astrological interpretation already established?
- Is the editorial layer organizing meaning rather than inventing meaning?
- Does the reading avoid claims the astrology does not support?
- Is uncertainty preserved when the signal is mixed or light?
