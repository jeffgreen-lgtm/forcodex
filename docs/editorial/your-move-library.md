# Your-Move Library

See also: [Field Guide](./field-guide.md), [Headline Library](./headline-library.md), [Notice-When Library](./notice-when-library.md), [Editorial Rules](./editorial-rules.md), [Taxonomy](./taxonomy.md)

## Rules for a Strong Your Move

- Give one concrete action.
- The action should be doable in under five minutes.
- Avoid “embrace,” “align,” “manifest,” and vague mindset advice.
- Prefer visible, measurable actions.
- The move should reduce noise, increase clarity, or create traction.

## Editorial Status

All examples below are initial editorial examples, not final production copy.

## Communication

- Rewrite your message in half the words before sending it.
- Ask one clarifying question before offering your opinion.
- Wait ten minutes before replying to the charged message.
- Put your main point in the first sentence.
- Delete one paragraph that only explains your anxiety.

## Relationships

- Send one honest sentence instead of a careful performance.
- Ask what the other person actually meant before reacting.
- Step away for five minutes before continuing the conversation.
- Name one need directly without packaging it as criticism.
- Let one exchange end without squeezing more certainty from it.

## Confidence

- Make one decision without asking for outside validation first.
- Straighten one part of your posture before the next conversation.
- Replace one apology with a clear statement.
- Finish one small task you have been circling.
- Say your point once without softening it three different ways.

## Decisions

- Cut one option from the list right now.
- Write down the simplest next step and do only that.
- Delay one non-urgent decision until tomorrow.
- Set a five-minute timer and choose before it ends.
- List one reason to proceed and one reason to pause.

## Work

- Pick the one task that changes the day if finished.
- Close every tab not needed for the next 20 minutes.
- Spend five minutes clearing the real blocker.
- Rename your top priority in one sentence.
- Finish one open loop before starting another.

## Creativity

- Draft the first ugly version now.
- Set a timer for five minutes and make a start.
- Save one idea before you judge it.
- Make one rough sketch instead of another plan.
- Continue the piece from the easiest entry point.

## Rest

- Step away from the screen for five full minutes.
- Drink water before pushing through the next task.
- Lie down or sit still without multitasking.
- Lower the noise in one part of your environment.
- Cancel one non-essential demand on your time.

## Boundaries

- Send the no without the extra paragraph.
- Put one delayed request on tomorrow’s list instead of today’s.
- Mute one thread that keeps pulling at your attention.
- State the limit in one sentence.
- Leave one message unanswered until you have real capacity.

## Growth

- Choose the response you have been avoiding once.
- Write down the repeating pattern in plain language.
- Make one new choice in the familiar situation.
- Name one cost of staying the same.
- Take the smallest action that proves change is real.

## Home

- Reset one surface before doing anything else.
- Put five things back where they belong.
- Handle the one household task you keep stepping around.
- Open a window or adjust the light in the room you are using.
- Spend five minutes restoring the part of home that affects you most.

## Money

- Check one account balance without opening five more tabs.
- Review the last three charges and label them.
- Move one purchase to tomorrow.
- Write down the next amount you actually need.
- Simplify one part of your plan before adding another rule.

## Purpose

- Do the next useful step instead of revisiting the whole plan.
- Spend five minutes on the work that matters most.
- Write one sentence naming what this season is really about.
- Remove one distraction from the path of meaningful work.
- Follow the clearest thread for five focused minutes.
