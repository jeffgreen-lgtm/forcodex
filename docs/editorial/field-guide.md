# CosmoScope Field Guide

## Purpose

The Field Guide translates astrological interpretation into practical, recognizable daily guidance.

It exists to help editors, writers, and future systems turn valid astrological meaning into clear user-facing reading structure without drifting into abstraction, prediction, or filler.

## How It Differs from the Canon

The [CosmoScope Canon](../cosmoscope-canon.md) governs company philosophy, product truth, and non-negotiable principles.

This Field Guide is narrower and operational. It governs how a reading is shaped on the page once the astrology and editorial intent are already defined.

## Reading Hierarchy

### Today’s Headline

The first answer.

It should name the day’s most useful human truth in plain language before any explanation begins.

### Notice When

The recognition layer.

It should describe moments the reader can realistically observe in a normal day, not symbolic abstractions or dramatic predictions.

### Your Move

The action layer.

It should offer one specific move the reader can make immediately, ideally in under five minutes.

### Why Today Feels This Way

The explanation layer.

It should explain the pattern clearly after the answer has already been delivered.

### Learn Your Sky

The educational layer.

It should help the reader understand how their chart and the current sky connect, without overwhelming the primary reading.

## Editorial QA Checklist

- Does the reading answer the user before explaining the astrology?
- Is the headline short, useful, and human?
- Are the “Notice When” moments observable and specific?
- Is “Your Move” concrete and immediately doable?
- Does the explanation clarify rather than inflate?
- Is the reading grounded in astrology rather than invented mood?
- Does every sentence earn its place?
- Does the reading preserve agency and reduce anxiety?
