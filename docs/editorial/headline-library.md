# Headline Library

See also: [Field Guide](./field-guide.md), [Notice-When Library](./notice-when-library.md), [Your-Move Library](./your-move-library.md), [Editorial Rules](./editorial-rules.md), [Taxonomy](./taxonomy.md)

## Rules for a Strong Headline

- Maximum 12 words.
- No planet names.
- No sign names.
- No mystical clichés.
- Must express one useful human truth.
- Must feel recognizable on contact.
- Must lead with clarity, not atmosphere.

## Editorial Status

All examples below are initial editorial examples, not final production copy.

## Communication

- Say less, mean more.
- Clarify before you commit.
- The real issue is how something is being said.
- A better question changes the whole conversation.
- Delay the reply until your thinking is cleaner.

## Relationships

- Closeness improves when you stop managing the whole exchange.
- A small honest moment changes more than a polished one.
- You do not need to fix the atmosphere alone.
- What feels personal may need perspective first.
- Let the conversation breathe before you define it.

## Confidence

- Confidence grows when you stop performing certainty.
- Trust the quieter version of your authority.
- You do not need a bigger display, just cleaner footing.
- Self-respect shows up as steadier pacing.
- Let confidence be precise, not loud.

## Decisions

- Not every choice needs to be made today.
- The cleanest decision has the least drag.
- Pause before calling urgency wisdom.
- A delayed answer may be the smarter answer.
- Narrow the choice before you force the outcome.

## Work

- Protect the work that actually moves things forward.
- Progress improves when the noise drops.
- The task is not bigger than your structure.
- Finish the real job before opening a new one.
- Useful work begins with a clearer priority.

## Creativity

- Make something small before you judge the whole direction.
- Momentum returns when perfection leaves the room.
- Creative clarity needs less pressure, not more.
- The next draft matters more than the ideal vision.
- Start where the energy is already alive.

## Rest

- Rest is part of the work now.
- Your pace needs a reset, not a push.
- Recovery makes the next step more accurate.
- Tired thinking is making ordinary things heavier.
- Slowing down may be the smartest move today.

## Boundaries

- A cleaner line prevents a louder problem.
- Boundaries work best before resentment builds.
- Protect your time before you explain it away.
- Not every demand deserves immediate access.
- A small no can preserve a better yes.

## Growth

- Growth begins where the old pattern stops working.
- Change becomes easier when you stop negotiating with it.
- The lesson is simpler than the resistance.
- What is repeating is asking for a new response.
- You are ready for a cleaner pattern.

## Home

- The atmosphere improves when one thing gets put back in order.
- Start by stabilizing the room around you.
- Home feels better when the friction gets named early.
- A calmer space changes the whole day.
- Tend the foundation before the next demand arrives.

## Money

- Spend with clarity, not reaction.
- A small review prevents a larger mess.
- Financial calm begins with one honest look.
- Keep the plan simple enough to follow.
- Money decisions improve when urgency drops.

## Purpose

- Meaning grows through action, not rumination.
- The next right move is more useful than the grand plan.
- Purpose becomes clearer when the noise leaves.
- What matters is asking for a cleaner lane.
- Direction returns when you stop scattering your attention.
